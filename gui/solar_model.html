Instructions

  1. Identify the local area which you wish to map
  2. Select a suitable map scale (1:200 recommended), and print out the chosen section of the map
  3. Using a highlighter or pen, outline the contour lines at suitable intervals depending on the level of detail desired (every 25 or 50 metres is recommended)
  4. Using the printed and drawn paper as a guideline, cut out each contour line layer on a fresh sheet of cardboard/foam
  5. Once the base layers are completed, separate hills/areas of elevation can be set aside
  6. Stick each layer together from the lowest elevation to the highest until all layers are built
  7. The 3D model is now completed and ready to be used or displayed!
